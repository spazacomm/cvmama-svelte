Millions of job seekers have asked themselves, “Can I just pay someone to find me a job?”

It’s a valid question. Job searching can be an extremely time consuming activity and also can cause a ton of unwanted stress.

Believe it or not, the answer is YES, you can pay someone to find you a job!

Just like you can hire a chef to cook you a meal, or a plumber to unclog your toilet, you can hire a job search professional to assist you with your job search.

But not all of these professionals are created equal. Some services can help you with a small part of your job search, like your resume or cover letter. While other services take over your entire job search, finding you jobs, applying to jobs for you, and setting you up with interviews.

In this article we will discuss the different types of service you can utilize to find you a job, as well as our top recommendations for the best companies that will find you a job.

Types of Agencies That Help Find Jobs

There are a few different types of companies that help find jobs. As mentioned above, these companies range in services and price so it’s important to know the difference.

Reverse Recruiters: If you are looking to actually hire someone to find you a job, that’s going to be a reverse recruiter. Reverse recruiters manage all aspects of your job search by finding, applying, and networking to jobs on your behalf. Think of them as a little clone of you, but with a ton of experience in job searching. While you work, spend time with family, or go out with your friends, your reverse recruiter will be sitting at home doing your entire job search for you. The only thing they cannot do it show up to the actual interviews for you.

Career Coaches: Career coaches are a much more scaled down solution where they won’t actually do the entire job search for you, but they will assist you with your job search. This is often times a more affordable option because they are teaching you how to job search, instead of doing all the work for you. If you’re interested in learning how to job search better on your own, career coaches might be your best bet.

Resume Writers: Resume writers are the least involved, but still very important. Every job search needs a quality resume. Resume writers are a good starting point when it comes to paying someone to help you find a job. A solid resume can make all the difference in a job search. If you later decide that you want additional assistance, or to outsource the actual job application process, career coaching or reverse recruiting might be worth considering down the road.

You might be wondering why recruiters and staffing agencies aren’t mentioned in this list. That’s because recruiting and staffing agencies cannot be hired by job seekers to help with a job search. Recruiters, headhunters, and staffing agencies are hired by the company to help fill positions, and are never hired by the job seeker to help with their job search.

Reverse Recruiters

These are the people we suspect you are really trying to find! 

Reverse recruiters take care of the entire job search process for you. This is the truest form of a person you can pay to find you a job.

Unlike traditional recruiters described above, reverse recruiters do not get paid by companies or hiring managers. Instead, they are paid by (and actively work for) you, the job seeker.

Reverse recruiters are trained in the art of job searching. It’s what they do full-time every day and, oh boy, does it make a difference!

You wouldn’t expect to be an expert at flying a jet after just one or two tries, would you? 

The same is true for job hunting. Rather than learning all the ins and outs of job searching, you can just pay an expert to do the work for you.

You can hire a reverse recruiter to help you with:

  • Finding highly targeted jobs that meet your criteria
  • Filling out daunting job applications on your behalf
  • Networking with key decision-makers
  • Preparing for interviews with expert advice

There are only a handful of companies that provide this service. Some companies will only fill out applications for you, while others will provide a more complete concierge-type service.

By far, reverse recruiters offer the most hands-on approach to job searching. They are a direct answer to the question, “Can I just pay someone to find me a job?”

With the immense value that they provide, don’t be surprised when you see services like this costing upwards of $1,499 per month.

Career Coaches

Next on the list we have career coaches. 

While not as hands-on as reverse recruiters, career coaches provide excellent coaching on all things job searching. 

You might be thinking, “I don’t want to pay someone to give me a bunch of homework!” And, I totally get it. But, hear me out.

While career coaches will require more of a time commitment on your end, they are a fraction of the cost of reverse recruiting services.

Rather than doing the work for you, career coaches are highly knowledgeable professionals who can help you avoid countless mistakes. This is a great option for anyone who wants expert advice and has the time to manage their job search on their own.

You could hire a career coach to help you with:

  • Discovering the right career
  • Job search strategies
  • Networking strategies
  • Interview preparation
  • Salary negotiation
  • Improving presentation skills and mental roadblocks
  • Providing encouragement and a sounding board during the process

Resume Writers

When it comes to paying someone to find a job, resume writers are usually the first people that come to mind. 

And for a good reason. There are more than 50,000 resume writers in the U.S. alone!

Resumes are a fundamental part of any job search. Whether we like it or not, most jobs won’t even let you apply without a resume.

You could hire a resume writer to help you with:

  • Resumes/CVs
  • Cover letters
  • LinkedIn profiles
  • Professional biographies
  • Personal statements

Resume writers are not all the same. Writing resumes that actually get results requires a thorough understanding of your work.

We stress this point more for resume writers than any other paid service simply because there are so many low-quality resume writers in the industry.

When you are looking for a resume writer, check for the following: experience in your line of work, number of edits/revisions, online reputation (reviews), and industry certifications.

By ensuring the basics above, you will save yourself countless headaches and disappointments.

Key Takeaways

As we mentioned at the beginning of this article, you can pay someone to help you find a job.

Reverse Recruiters, career coaches, and resume writers are the best options to hire for your job search.

Find My Profession is our top pick for companies that provide reverse recruiting, career coaching, and resume writing services all in one place.

Whatever option you choose, remember to stay positive. Your job search is temporary and you don’t have to do it alone!
